Human Rights

Introduction

Human rights are fundamental rights and freedoms that every person is entitled to, regardless of nationality, sex, age, ethnicity, religion, or any other status.

Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR)

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights, adopted by the United Nations General Assembly in 1948, is a milestone document that proclaims the inalienable rights that everyone is entitled to as a human being.

Key Human Rights

  • Right to Life: Everyone has the right to life, liberty, and security of person.
  • Freedom of Expression: Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression.
  • Equality: All individuals are equal before the law and are entitled to equal protection.

Protecting Human Rights

It is essential to work towards protecting and promoting human rights globally. Governments, organizations, and individuals play a crucial role in ensuring the dignity and well-being of all people.
